I joined Sanctuary AI as a software engineer in 2021 fresh after completing my MSc. This was a huge step for me, as my background up to that point was mostly mechanical oriented, with most of my programming / robotics experience being self-driven. Shortly afterwards I became the first member of the teleoperation team. Teleoperation has been a great fit for me, as it sits in the center of many different disciplines which suits my generalist skill-set quite well. Hardware, software, controls, and networking can all have substantial impact on teleoperation performance. You really have to have perspective on all of these areas to enable high performing teleoperation through dexterous manipulation. Here's a link to Sanctuary's Robots Doing Stuff playlist, with over 50 videos of my team demonstrating some of the awesome humanoid teleoperation of some fairly difficult tasks.
